# Spring-Supported Laboratory Shaker Design

This project was developed as a school project focused on the design of a laboratory shaker mechanism. The main objective was to create a functional and manufacturable shaker system by using a predefined motor and designing the remaining mechanical structure according to the required motion, stability, and production constraints.

## Project Purpose

The purpose of this project is to design a laboratory shaker that can securely hold a sample container with a spring-supported holding system and provide a controlled shaking motion. The system was designed to operate at different angular positions, approximately 10°, 15°, and 20°, depending on the required shaking angle.

The motor was provided as a fixed component, and the entire mechanical design was developed around the motor’s motion, mounting position, and functional requirements.

## Bearing and Fixation Design

In the bearing connection area, set screws were used to compress and secure the bearing in position. This design choice was made to improve the stability of the rotating mechanism and reduce unwanted movement during operation.

The bearing support region was designed carefully to keep the rotating parts aligned and to provide a more reliable motion transfer from the motor to the shaker mechanism.

## Manufacturing and Design Considerations

Manufacturability was an important part of the design process. Since the project was planned with 3D printing in mind, the geometry and structural details were designed according to the limitations of the 3D printer.

The design was shaped by considering:

- 3D printer size and production limits
- Part strength and printability
- Assembly feasibility
- Support and connection points
- Weight distribution
- Structural stability during motion

Due to these constraints, several design decisions were made to make the system easier to manufacture, assemble, and operate.

## Engineering Approach

The design process included weight calculations and evaluation of risky design decisions. Critical areas were reviewed and improved to reduce possible failure points. The structure was strengthened at important connection regions to increase durability during the shaking motion.

The main engineering focus was to create a stable, functional, and producible laboratory shaker while working within the given motor specifications and 3D printing limitations.
